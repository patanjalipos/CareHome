Assess residents’ ability to smoke independently

Information Sources

Would you like to stop smoking

How many cigarettes do you smoke every day

When is your first cigarette of the day

Do you need assistance smoking

Please add assistance details

How often have you fallen asleep while smoking a cigarette

Please indicate: Never, Number of times

What would you do if there was an emergency in the smoking room/area

Resident should say unaided, that they would call for help and leave the area

Note: If resident has fallen asleep more than once while smoking or is unable to answer What would you do if there was an emergency in the smoking room/area? correctly, then resident is a fire risk.

Risk Factors

Are there any physical limitations, which have implications on the resident’s ability to smoke (i.e. arthritis, hand injury, paralysis

Does the resident use assistive devices that may alter his/her ability to smoke (i.e. splints/neck collar)

Is the resident known to engage in the following unsafe smoking practices

Is the resident known to attempt to set fires or use ignition materials unsafely

Does the resident have a history of falling asleep while seated

Does the resident take any medication which could affect his/her ability to smoke safely (e.g. cause drowsiness)

Is the resident incontinent

If YES to any of the above: The resident is a fire risk and not safe to smoke independently. If it appears the resident is safe to smoke independently an observation must be done as follows

Direct Observation

Can the resident get to the smoking room

Can the resident obtain cigarettes and lighter

Can the resident access an ashtray

Can the resident light a cigarette

Can the resident hold a cigarette securely

Can the resident dispose of ashes in ashtray

Can the resident put out a cigarette

Is the resident able to call for emergency assistance

If NO to any of the above, resident is a fire risk and not safe to smoke independently
