Depressed or despairing

The resident is frequently in a state of misery, grief or upset, a downcast expression, makes little eye contact. The resident says things that indicate feelings of hopelessness and helplessness. The resident quickly returns to this state after happier interludes. The resident cries a lot

Intensely angry or aggressive

The resident has outbursts of rage that go beyond expressing appropriate feelings. e.g. unprovoked attacks on other people. Howls of rage when things go wrong can be seen as signs of well-being e.g. the person is attempting to communicate or is expressing appropriate feelings

Shows anxiety or fear

The resident appears worried, anxious, apprehensive etc. e.g. continual nervous movements, stuttering speech, trembling, repetitive actions. They may show signs of panic, nervous trembling, rapid stuttering, crouching in a fetal position

Agitated or restless

The person shows signs of agitation such as more movement than usual and or faster movement than usual. This also applies to speech, which is faster and more frequent than usual. Also look out for worried frowns. If a person is agitated, repetitive movements become faster

Withdrawn or listless

The resident does very little and is reluctant to join in activities. The resident does not seem alert, drifts in and out of sleep and does not respond to stimulation. The resident is not active

Has physical discomfort or pain

The resident shows signs of real or imagined pain e.g. wincing, rubbing or holding the sore place. They may say that some part of the body hurts but may not say anything. Watch out for signs that the resident is in pain

Unresolved grieving over losses

Grief over all the things that the person has lost is appropriate for someone moving into long-term care from own home. They have lost many things e.g. independence, status, being with loved ones, abilities, maybe they have lost a partner through death.

Bodily tension

The person shows a lot of bodily tension or adopts rigid positions. The resident is rarely relaxed

Easily walked over by other people

The person is put off or crushed by other people's remarks or actions. The resident creeps away if someone makes a critical remark or they stop doing an activity if someone else is watching. The resident does not defend themselves if someone takes something that belongs to them

Feels like an outsider

The resident is living in a place where they are at risk of feeling like an outsider. e.g. their first language is not English, living in a place where everyone else is an English speaker. The only member of the group who follows a strict religious code

Scores more than 12 indicate some cause for concern about the well-being of the person
